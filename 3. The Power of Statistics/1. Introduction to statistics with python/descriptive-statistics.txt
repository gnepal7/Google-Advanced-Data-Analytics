# Measures of central tendency
  + Measures of central tendency
    - Values that represent the center of a dataset
  + Measures of dispersion
    - Values that represent the spread of a dataset

  + Measures of center tendency are:
    1. Mean
      - Average values of a dataset
      mean = sum of all values / total number of all values
    2. Median
      - Middle values of a dataset
      median = 1, 5, 8, 9, 10 => 8
    + When to use median of mean:
      - If outliers, use median
      - If no outliers, use mean
    3. Mode
      - The most frequently occuring value in a dataset

# Measures of central tendency: The mean, the median, and the mode
  + When to use the mean, the median, and the mode 
  + Mean versus median
   . Mean and median both measure central tendency.
   . Mean is sensitive to outliers in data.
   . Median is better when outliers are present.
  + Mode
   . Mode identifies the most frequently occurring category.
   . Useful for analyzing categorical survey response data.

# Measures of dispersion: Range, variance, and standard deviation
  + Range 
    - The range is the difference between the largest and smallest value in a dataset.
  + Variance
    - Variance measures how spread out data values are.
    - Variance is the square of standard deviation.
  + Standard deviation
    - Standard deviation measures data spread around the mean.
    - Larger values mean more spread; smaller values mean less spread.

# Measures of position
  - Determine the position of a value in relation to other values
    in a dataset
  + Measures of position:
    - Percentiles
      . The value below which a percentage of data falls
    - Quartiles
      . Divides the values into 4 equal dataset, each include 25%
    - Interquartile range (IQR)
      . The distance between the first quartile(Q1) and 3rd quartile(Q3)
    - Five number summary
      . The minimum
      . The first quartile (Q1)
      . The median, or second quartile(Q2)
      . The third quartile
      . The maximum
