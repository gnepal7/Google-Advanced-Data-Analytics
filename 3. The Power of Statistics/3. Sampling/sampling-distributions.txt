# Infer population parameters with the central limit theorem 
  + The central limit theorem 
    - Larger sample sizes make sampling distributions approach normality.
    - Sample mean gets closer to population mean as size increases.
    - Large representative samples estimate population means accurately.
  + Conditions
    - Randomization: Every population member has an equal selection chance.
    - Independence: One observation’s value does not affect another.
    - Sample size: The sample size needs to be sufficiently large. Depends on the following           factors:
      . Precision requirement: Larger samples improve normality and estimate accuracy.
      . Population shape: Bell-shaped populations need smaller samples for normality.

# The sampling distribution of the mean
  + Sampling distribution of the sample mean 
    - Sampling distribution: Probability distribution of a sample statistic.
    - Sample statistics: Include mean, median, standard deviation, and range.
    - Purpose: Estimate population parameters using sample data.
    - Repeated sampling: Different samples produce different statistic values.
    - Sampling distribution of mean: Distribution of all sample means.
  + Standard error
    - Standard error: Standard deviation of a sample statistic.
    - Standard error measures sampling variability numerically.
    - Standard error of the mean measures variability among sample means.
    - Larger standard error indicates greater spread and variability.
    - Estimated formula: standard error = s / √n.
