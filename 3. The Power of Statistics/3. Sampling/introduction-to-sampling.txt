# The sampling process
  1. Identify the target population
    - The complete set of elements that you're interested in knowing
      more about
    - It is general
  2. Select the sampling frame
    - A list of all the items in your target population
    - It is specific
  3. Choose the sampling method
    - Probability sampling :- Uses random selection to generate a sample
    - Non-probability samplint :- Based on convenience or personal preference
    - Probability sampling uses random selection for inclusion.
    - Each population element has equal chance, improving representativeness
  4. Determine the sample size
    - The number of individuals or items chosen for a study or experiment
    - The more sample size, the more accurate result will occurs
  5. Collect the sample data
    - Survey collects customer feedback on refrigerator digital features
    - Results help stakeholders decide future product investments and features

# Compare sampling methods
  + Probability sampling methods:
    1. Simple random sampling
      - Every member of a population is selected randomly and has an equal
        chance of being chosen.
      . Representative
      . Aviod bias
    2. Stratified random sampling
      - Divide a population into groups and randomly select some members
        from each group to be in the sample.
      - These groups are called strata.
    3. Cluster random sampling
      - Divide a population into clusters, randomly select certain clusters,
        and include all members from the chosen clusters in the sample
      . Sample will be selected by each clusters
    4. Systematic random sampling
      - Put every member of a population into an ordered sequence. Then, you choose a 
        random starting point in the sequence and select members for your 
        sample at regular intervals.
      . Representative
      . Quick and convenient

# The impact of bias in sampling (Non-probability)
  + Sampling bias
    - When a sample is not representative of the population as a whole
  + Non-probability sampling methods:
    1. Convenience sampling
      - choose members of a population that are easy to contact or reach
      - Undercoverage bias :- When some members of a population are 
        inadequately represented in the sample.
    2. Voluntary response sampling
      - Consists of members of a population who volunteer to participate in a study.
      - Nonresponse bias :- When certain groups of people are less likely to provide responses.
    3. Snowball sampling
      - Researchers recruit initial participants to be in a study and then ask them
        to recruit other people to participate in the study.
    4. Purposive sampling
      - Researchers select participants based on the purpose of their study.

# Non-probability sampling methods
  - Non-probability sampling is useful with limited time and resources.
  - Best for exploratory research, not population-wide conclusions.
  - Watch for sampling bias and ensure fairness throughout analysis.
