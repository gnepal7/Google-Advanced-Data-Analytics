# Introduction to confidence intervals
  + Point estimate
    - Uses a single value to estimate a population parameter
  + Confidence interval
    . Sample statistic
    . Margin of error
      - The maximum expected difference between a population parameter and a sample estimate.
      - Formula, margin of error = z-score * SE
    . Confidence level
      - Describes the liklihood that a particular sampling method will produce a confidence 
        interval that includes the population parameter
      - Common levels are 90%, 95%, and 99%

# Interpret confidence intervals
  + Confidence level
    - Expresses the uncertainity of the estimation process.

# Confidence intervals: Correct and incorrect interpretations
  + Correct interpretation  
    - Confidence intervals estimate the population mean using sample data.
    - 95% confidence refers to the method’s long-term success rate.
    - About 95% of repeated intervals would contain the population mean.
    - Approximately 5% of intervals would miss the population mean.
    - Confidence intervals quantify uncertainty caused by random sampling.
  + Incorrect interpretations
    - Misinterpretation 1: 95% is not the probability population mean is inside interval.
    - Reason: Population mean is a fixed constant, not random.
    - Correct interpretation: 95% of repeated intervals capture the population mean.
    - Confidence level: Refers to the estimation method’s long-term success rate.
    - Misinterpretation 2: 95% does not mean population data fall in interval.
    - Reason: Confidence intervals estimate the mean, not individual data values.
    - Misinterpretation 3: 95% does not mean sample means fall in interval.
    - Reason: More than 5% of sample means may lie outside.
