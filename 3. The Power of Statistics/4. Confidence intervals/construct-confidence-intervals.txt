# Construct a confidence interval for a proportion
  1. Identify a sample statistic
    - proportion
  2. Choose a confidence level
    - 95%
  3. Find the margin of error
    - margin of error = z-score * SE => 1.96 in 2nd standard deviation
      > 1.96 * 0.05 = 0.098
  4. Calculate the intervel
    - Upper limit = sample proportion + margin of error
                  0.55 + 0.098 = 0.648 = 64.8%
    - Lower limit = 0.55 - 0.098 = 0.452 = 45.2%
    - Confidence interval:
      95% Cl [45.2, 64.8]

# Construct a confidence interval for a mean

    - mean
  2. Choose a confidence level
    95%

# Construct a confidence interval for a small sample size
  + Large versus small sample sizes
    - Large sample: Z-scores
      . Large samples use z-scores to calculate margin of error.
      . Central limit theorem makes sample means approximately normally distributed.
    - Small sample: T-scores 
      . Small sample sizes use the t-distribution instead of the normal distribution.
      . Use t-scores when data is approximately normally distributed.
