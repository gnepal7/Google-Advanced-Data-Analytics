# One-sample test for means
  + One-sample test
  - Determines whether or not a population parameter like a mean 
    or proportion is equal to a specific value.
  + Two-sample test
    - Determines whether or not two population parameters such as two
      means or two proportions are equal to each other

  + One-sample z-test assumptions
    - The data is a random sample of a normally distributed population
    - The population standard deviation is known
  + Test statistic 
    - A value that shows how closely your observed data matches the
      distribution expected under the null hypotheses
  + Z-score
    - A measure of how many standard deviations below or above
      the population mean a data point is
