# Correlation versus causation: Interpret regression results
  + What is correlation?
    - correlation measures the way two variables tend to change together.
    - Positive correlation is a relationship between two variables that 
      tend to increase or decrease together.
    - Negative correlation is an inverse relationship between two variables, 
      where when one variable increases, the other variable tends to decrease, and vice versa.

  + It’s difficult to claim causation
    - Causation describes a cause-and-effect relationship where one variable directly 
      causes the other to change in a particular way
