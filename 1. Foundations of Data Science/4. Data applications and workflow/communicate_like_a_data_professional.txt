# Connect PACE with upcoming course themes

# The value of the PACE strategy document
 + Why do I need the PACE strategy document?
  - Program builds foundational and advanced data skills.
  - Success requires both technical and communication skills.
  - Data professionals support organizational decision-making.
  - Stakeholder communication is a key responsibility.
  - Courses are structured in a logical learning progression.
  - Each course includes an end-of-course project.
  - Projects demonstrate analytical and communication mastery.
  - PACE strategy documents guide course project work.
  - They include tips and reflection opportunities.
  - Documents help create executive summaries for stakeholders.
  - They track progress and improve communication skills over time.

 + A look at the PACE strategy document
  - Instructions :- general guidelines and special considerations
  - Course project recap
  - Relevant interview questions
  - Review relevant course materials
  - Reference guide
  - Data project questions and considerations

 + PACE strategy documents and Jupyter notebooks
  - Each end-of-course project includes a Jupyter notebook with programming guidance and tips.
  - Notebooks and strategy documents both include guiding questions, sometimes overlapping.
  - These resources help create executive summaries for stakeholders during project execution.

 + The benefits of the PACE strategy documents
  - PACE documents help overcome challenges in building a portfolio.
  - Recording decisions and reflections increases future value of your work.
  - They provide material for a cohesive, professional portfolio over time.

# Communicate objectives with a project proposal
 + Project proposal
  - Project proposals define objectives and requirements.
  - They break ideas into actionable milestones.
  - They are created with input from stakeholders and teams.
  - Proposals are shared with clients or executives for approval.
  - They are widely used across many industries.
  - Key elements are generally consistent despite design differences.
  => Elements of a project proposal
   . Project title
   . Project objectives
   . Milestones
   . Tasks
   . Outcomes
   . Deliverables
   . Stakeholders
   . Estimated time

# Connect PACE with executive summaries
 + Executive summaries
  - Executive summaries highlight key project points for decision-makers.
  - They help stakeholders and new team members quickly understand projects.
  - They are designed for busy executives who need concise information.
  - Executive summaries are used across many industries.
  - Formats vary, but common key elements exist.
  => Elements of an executive summary
   . Project title
   . The problem
   . The solution
   . Details/key insights
   . Next steps/Recommendations
