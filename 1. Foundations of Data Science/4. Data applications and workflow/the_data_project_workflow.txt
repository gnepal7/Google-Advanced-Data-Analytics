# Introduction to PACE
 "By failing to prepare, ​you are preparing to fail."
  - Benjamin Franklin
 + Data workflow structure:-
  - Ask
  - Prepare
  - Process
  - Analyze
  - Share
  - Act
 + PACE Framework includes:
  => Plan
   - What are the goals of the project?
   - What strategies will be needed?
   - What will be the business or operational impacts?
  => Analyze
   - Acquire data from primary and secondary sources
   - Clean, reorganize, and transform data for analysis
   - Engage in Exploretory data analysis (EDA)
   - Work with stakeholders
  => Construct
   - Build and reviese machine learning models
   - Uncover relationships in the data
   - Apply statistical inferences about data relationships
  => Execute
   - Present finding to internal and external stakeholders
   - Answer questions
   - Consider differing viewpoints\
   - Present recommendations based on the data

# The PACE Stages
 => A closer look at the PACE model
 + Plan :- conceptualize the scope of the project and develop the steps
  . Research business data
  . Define the project scope
  . Develop a workflow
  . Assess project and/or stakeholder needs
 + Analyze :- Collect, prepare, and analyze all of the data for project.
  . Format database
  . Scrub data
  . Convert data into usable formats
 + Construct :- Build models that will allow you access to hidden relationships locked within data
  . Select modeling approach
  . Build models
  . Build machine learning algorithms
 + Execute :- Present the finding of your analysis
  . Share results
  . Present findings to other stakeholders
  . Address feedback

  - Communication and PACE 
   . Communication is essential throughout all PACE workflow stages.
   . It supports tasks like asking questions, gathering data, and updating stakeholders.
   . Feedback and communication help improve progress and final results.
  
  - Adaptability of PACE
   . PACE begins with planning, gathering information and setting structure.
   . Analysis and construction support building models and interpreting data.
   . Execution involves sharing results and collecting stakeholder feedback.
   . PACE is flexible and not strictly linear in order.
   . Communication allows movement between stages as needed.
