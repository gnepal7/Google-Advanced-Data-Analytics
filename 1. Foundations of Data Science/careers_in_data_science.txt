# Data discourse over the years
- What is data science vs. data analytics
 . Data science is an entire field dedicated to making data more useful.
 . Data analytics is a subfield of the larger data science discipline.
 
 => Data science 
 . Produces broad insights that concentrate on which questions should be asked about data
 . Confronts what is unknown by using advanced techniques to make predictions about the future 
 . Seeks to answer questions like “What will happen?” and “How can we make X happen?”
 
 => Data analytics
  . Emphasizes discovering answers to questions being asked 
  . Determines actionable insights that can be applied immediately based on existing queries
  . Seeks to answer questions like “What happened?” and “Why did it happen?”

- The connections between data science and data analytics
  . Data science and analytics both uncover insights for organizational growth.
  . Both rely on measurable real-world data and interactions.
  . Data teams combine analysts and scientists for collaborative projects.
  . Similar tools, software, and programming languages are commonly used.
  . Job roles and titles often overlap across industries and organizations.

- The data career space over time
 . In 1965, the world’s population was 3.3 billion. The Mariner 4 spacecraft took the first 
   close-up pictures of Mars. The first commercial communications satellite allowed nearly 
   instantaneous contact between Europe and North America.
   + Technology:-
    - Early computations shifted from manual work to programmable calculators.
   + Data Storage:-
    - Punch cards and magnetic tapes stored growing business data.
   + Data Analysis
    - Smaller, cheaper computers increased company adoption of data analysis.
   + Communication:-
    - Electric typewriters improved document creation and office correspondence.
    - Advanced telephone systems enhanced communication within organizations.
 
 . In 1985, the world’s population was 4.8 billion.
   + Technology : Fax machines and personal computers transformed business communication.
   + Data storage : Floppy disks and hard drives improved data storage capacity.
   + Data analysis : Electronic spreadsheets revolutionized financial data organization and analysis.
   + Communication : Email enabled faster communication among organizations and institutions.
 
 . In 2005, the world’s population was 6.5 billion.
   + Technology : High-speed internet and smart devices expanded global connectivity.
   + Data storage : Cloud services and flash drives improved digital data storage.
   + Data analysis : Predictive modeling and unstructured data analytics became essential.
   + Communication : Smart devices enabled global collaboration, email, and instant messaging.
 
 . Today the world’s population is around 8 billion
   + Technology : AR, VR, IoT, and edge computing enhance digital experiences.
   + Data storage : Quantum computing aims to revolutionize processing and storage capabilities.
   + Data analysis : Artificial intelligence accelerates innovation and human decision-making abilities.
   + Communication : Virtual meetings and task software support global asynchronous collaboration.
