# The data professional career space
 - Data tasks and responsibilities are dependent on an organization's
   data, team structure, and how they make use of insights and analytics.

 + Common data professional job titles
  - Data analyst
  - Data scientist
  - Responsibilities
   . Look for patterns and trends within big datasets
   . Uncover the stories inside data
   . Help guide decision making
   . Translate key information into visualizations

 + Other data professional job titles
  - Junior data scientist
  - Data scientist-entry level
  - Associate data scientist
  - Data science associate

 + Roles that use data and analytical skills
  - Data engineer
  - Insights or analytics team manager
  - Business intelligence engineer or analyst

 + Data engineer responsibilities:
  - Make data accessible
  - Ensure data ecosystem produces reliable results
  - Deal with infrastructure for data across enterprise

 + Insights or analytic team manager responsibilities:
  - Supervise analytical strategy of organization
  - Manage multiple groups

# Build the perfect data team
 + Five principles for data team building
  1. Adaptability
   . Data platforms should be flexible for different tools and preferences.
   . Remote and on-site teams need open access to shared data resources.
   . Allowing tool flexibility broadens hiring and improves team diversity.
   . Organizations benefit from adaptable and inclusive data environments.
   . Data professionals must continuously learn new skills and technologies.
  2. Activation
   . Data analysis requires skilled and experienced data professionals.
   . Organizations should build data literacy through daily habits and routines.
   . Dashboards improve access to data and support collaboration.
   . Interpersonal skills are essential for successful data culture transformation.
   . Data professionals help promote understanding across the organization.
  3. Standardization
   . Standardizing data practices improves communication and consistency.
   . Shared assets and code increase efficiency and save development time.
   . Collaboration helps embed best practices into organizational culture.
   . Innovation is important for developing effective best practices.
   4. Accountability
   . Data analysis requires strong accountability and responsibility.
   . A clear “paper trail” ensures transparency and process tracking.
   . Accountability improves explainability, security, and alignment with goals.
   . Metadata and records help teams analyze and adjust models.
   . Communication throughout workflows supports organizational accountability.
  5. Business impact
   . Data projects can fail when business impact is unclear.
   . Planning should consider all possible data solutions and constraints.
   . Factors like resources, integration, and timelines must be evaluated.
   . Broadening project scope improves solution quality over time.
   . Trust in data teams is built through communication and results.

 - RACI :- Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed
